SOUR PATCH, soft candy with a coating of invert sugar and sour sugar


Sour Patch Kids were originally created by Frank Galatolie of Jaret International, under the name of Mars Men in the early 1970s.

In the late 1970s, Cadbury and the Smeera Blyton Licorice Company of Sweden formed the Allen Candy Company in Hamilton, Ontario, to produce them.

In 1985 they re-branded to Sour Patch Kids, but it is now owned by Santi.

The name was likely changed to capitalize on the popularity of Cabbage Patch Kids.

The slogans "Sour Then Sweet" and "Sour. Sweet. Gone." refers to the candy's sour-to-sweet taste.
