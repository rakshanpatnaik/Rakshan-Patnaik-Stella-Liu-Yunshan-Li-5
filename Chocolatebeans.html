M&MS®

ALLERGY INFORMATION: CONTAINS PEANUTS, MILK AND SOY. MAY CONTAIN TREE NUTS.


Multi-colored, button-shaped, each has the letter "m" printed in white on one side, consisting of a candy shell surrounding a filling which varies depending upon the variety of M&M's.


The candy originated in the United States in 1941, and M&M's have been sold in over 100 countries since 2003.

They are produced in different colors, and has breeded many varvarieties, some made available for a limited time, such as white cheesecake for Easter, pumpkin spice or white candy corn for Halloween, and White Strawberry Shortcake for Valentine's Day.

The candy-coated chocolate concept was inspired by a method used to allow soldiers in the Spanish Civil War (1936–1939) to carry chocolate in warm climates without it melting.

The company's longest-lasting slogan reflects this: "Melts in your mouth, not in your hand."
